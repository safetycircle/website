Leading Indicators

Hazards Reported

This indicator measures the volume of one way the workforce is ‘speaking-up’ about WHS issues.

Hazards Closed

Closing out reported hazards effectively is a measure of WHS leadership AND a key measure of practical risk management in the organisation.

Near-Hits Reported

This is a simple yet complex measure. We want all near-hits with significant or high risk potential to be reported.

WHS Corrective Actions

Corrective Actions are derived from hazards reported; workplace inspection findings; audit recommendations and outcomes; near-hit reports; incident reports and investigations.

Hazard to Incident Ratio

High Reliability and Ultra Reliability organisations report many more hazards then incidents.

WHS Inspections

Responsible WHS leaders ensure that WHS inspections are performed in their work areas as another proactive way to identify hazards and issues before they manifest into incidents, injuries and ill-health.

Lagging Indicators

Incidents vs Claims vs LTIs

Incidents Reports are critical to identifying existing issues where the workforce is losing risk control. Claims arise from incidents. Lost Time Injuries (LTIs) are a subset of those claims.

LTIFR & LTISR

Lost Time Injury Frequency Rate (LTIFR) is the number of LTIs that have occurred per million hours worked, in the past 12 months. This is a ‘rolling 12 month average’.

Sprains and Strains

This graph shows the number and average cost of strain & sprain injuries reported by the workers compensation insurer in the past month.

Falls and Muscular Stress

This graph shows the number and average cost of both Fall -related claims and Muscular Stress-related claims in the “Mechanism of Injury” category of the claims data.
